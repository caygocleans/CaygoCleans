CATEGORY 1: KITCHEN (12 Solutions)
════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. All-Purpose Surface Spray
   Ingredients: 1 cup white vinegar + 1 cup water + 10 drops essential oil
   How: Mix in a spray bottle. Spray and wipe with a microfibre cloth.

2. Stovetop Degreaser
   Ingredients: Baking soda + a few drops of dish soap
   How: Mix into a paste. Apply to stovetop, leave 5 minutes, wipe clean.

3. Oven Deep Cleaner
   Ingredients: Baking soda + white vinegar
   How: Coat oven interior with baking soda paste overnight. Spray with white
   vinegar the next morning. Let it fizz, then wipe away completely.

4. Microwave Steam Clean
   Ingredients: Bowl of water + half a lemon
   How: Microwave on high for 3 minutes. Let steam sit 2 minutes. Wipe walls
   clean — food and grease lifts off with no scrubbing.

5. Fridge Deodoriser
   Ingredients: Baking soda (open box or small dish)
   How: Place inside fridge. Replace every 3 months. Continuously absorbs odours.

6. Drain Unclogger
   Ingredients: ½ cup baking soda + ½ cup white vinegar + boiling water
   How: Pour baking soda down drain, follow with vinegar. Let fizz 5 minutes.
   Flush with boiling water. Repeat weekly to prevent buildup.

7. Cutting Board Sanitiser
   Ingredients: Coarse salt + half a lemon
   How: Sprinkle salt over board. Scrub with cut lemon in circular motions.
   Rinse with cold water. Eliminates bacteria and food odours.

8. Cabinet Grease Remover
   Ingredients: Warm water + dish soap + white vinegar
   How: Mix in equal parts, apply with microfibre cloth. Wipe handles and
   cabinet fronts — these collect more grease than anywhere else in the kitchen.

9. Stainless Steel Polisher
   Ingredients: A few drops of olive oil
   How: Apply to a dry cloth and buff in the direction of the grain. Removes
   smudges and leaves a protective shine.

10. Dishwasher Cleaner
    Ingredients: 1 cup white vinegar
    How: Place cup upright on the top rack. Run a full hot cycle with no dishes.
    Clears buildup and eliminates odours monthly.

11. Fridge Interior Cleaner
    Ingredients: Equal parts water + white vinegar
    How: Mix in a spray bottle. Spray all interior surfaces and shelves.
    Wipe with a clean cloth. Kills bacteria, no chemical residue on food.

12. Kettle Descaler
    Ingredients: White vinegar + water (50/50)
    How: Half-fill kettle with solution. Boil. Pour out. Rinse twice with
    fresh water before use. Removes limescale buildup completely.

CATEGORY 2: BATHROOM (11 Solutions)
════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Toilet Bowl Cleaner
   Ingredients: ½ cup baking soda + ½ cup white vinegar
   How: Pour baking soda into bowl, follow with vinegar. Let fizz 10 minutes.
   Scrub with toilet brush and flush.

2. Grout Cleaner
   Ingredients: Baking soda + water (paste)
   How: Apply paste directly to grout lines with an old toothbrush. Scrub in
   circular motions. Rinse thoroughly. For heavy mould, add white vinegar.

3. Mould Remover
   Ingredients: Undiluted white vinegar
   How: Spray directly onto mould. Leave for 1 full hour — do not rinse early.
   Scrub with a brush and rinse. The acidity kills mould at the root.

4. Shower Glass Descaler
   Ingredients: White vinegar + a few drops dish soap
   How: Mix and spray onto glass. Leave 5 minutes. Wipe with a squeegee or
   microfibre cloth in S-strokes. Removes hard water mineral deposits.

5. Soap Scum Remover
   Ingredients: Dish soap + white vinegar (1:1)
   How: Combine in a spray bottle, shake gently. Spray onto tiles and glass,
   leave 5 minutes, then scrub with a cloth or sponge.

6. Drain Hair + Smell Fix
   Ingredients: Baking soda + white vinegar
   How: First, pull out hair manually (use gloves). Then treat weekly with
   baking soda + vinegar flush as per the kitchen drain method above.

7. Mirror Cleaner (Streak-Free)
   Ingredients: 1 part white vinegar + 1 part water
   How: Spray onto a cloth (not the mirror). Wipe in a top-to-bottom S-pattern.
   Never in circles — that causes streaks.

8. Toilet Freshener Tabs (DIY)
   Ingredients: 1 cup baking soda + ½ cup citric acid + essential oil drops
   How: Mix dry ingredients. Add 10–15 drops essential oil. Press into an
   ice cube tray or silicone mould. Dry overnight. Drop one in the bowl weekly.

9. Tile Deep Cleaner
   Ingredients: Baking soda + dish soap
   How: Make a thick paste. Apply to tiles. Scrub with a stiff brush.
   Rinse with hot water for full removal.

10. Showerhead Descaler
    Ingredients: White vinegar (enough to submerge)
    How: Fill a plastic bag with vinegar. Secure around showerhead with a rubber
    band so the head is fully submerged. Leave overnight. Remove and run hot water.

11. Bathroom Air Freshener (Natural)
    Ingredients: Small dish of baking soda + 5 drops essential oil
    How: Stir together and place in a corner of the bathroom. Absorbs odours
    passively. Refresh the essential oil every week.

CATEGORY 3: LIVING SPACES (11 Solutions)
════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. All-Purpose Surface Spray
   Same as Kitchen #1 — white vinegar + water + essential oil.

2. Wood Furniture Polish
   Ingredients: 2 tbsp olive oil + 1 tbsp lemon juice
   How: Mix together. Apply a small amount to a soft cloth. Buff into wood
   surfaces. Restores shine and conditions the wood naturally.

3. Glass & Window Cleaner
   Ingredients: 1 part white vinegar + 1 part water
   How: Spray on cloth, not glass. Wipe in a top-to-bottom S-pattern.
   Best done in shade — direct sunlight causes fast drying and streaks.

4. Carpet Deodoriser
   Ingredients: Baking soda + optional essential oil drops
   How: Sprinkle generously over carpet. Leave for at least 1 hour (overnight
   for strong odours). Vacuum thoroughly. Instantly freshens carpet fibres.

5. Fabric Sofa Refresher
   Ingredients: Baking soda + white vinegar (diluted spray)
   How: Sprinkle baking soda over sofa. Leave 30 minutes. Vacuum off.
   For lingering smells, lightly mist with diluted vinegar and allow to air dry.

6. Hardwood Floor Cleaner
   Ingredients: ½ cup white vinegar per gallon of warm water
   How: Damp mop only — never saturate wood. Excess moisture warps and
   damages hardwood over time. No rinsing needed.

7. Tile & Vinyl Floor Cleaner
   Ingredients: A few drops of dish soap in a warm water bucket
   How: Mop as normal. No need to rinse. Safe on all hard floor types.

8. Upholstery Stain Remover
   Ingredients: 1 part dish soap + 1 part hydrogen peroxide
   How: Mix and blot onto stain with a clean cloth. Leave 5 minutes. Blot
   (never rub). Rinse with cold water. ALWAYS test on a hidden area first.

9. Passive Odour Absorber
   Ingredients: Open dish of baking soda OR activated charcoal bag
   How: Place in corners, under furniture, or inside wardrobes. Works
   continuously without any sprays or plugins.

10. Dusty Blinds Cleaner
    Ingredients: Old sock + vinegar water solution
    How: Slip the sock over your hand like a glove. Dip in diluted white
    vinegar. Slide over each slat front and back. Fast and thorough.

11. TV / Screen Cleaner
    Ingredients: Isopropyl alcohol (70%) + microfibre cloth
    How: Apply alcohol lightly to the cloth — never spray directly on the
    screen. Gently wipe in one direction. No circular motions.

CATEGORY 4: LAUNDRY (10 Solutions)
════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Natural Laundry Detergent (DIY)
   Ingredients: Washing soda + baking soda + grated castile soap bar
   How: Mix equal parts. Use 1 tablespoon per load. Works in cold and warm water.
   Gentle on skin, safe for sensitive skin and children's clothing.

2. Natural Fabric Softener
   Ingredients: White vinegar
   How: Add ½ cup to the rinse cycle compartment. Softens fabric and removes
   detergent residue. No vinegar smell remains after drying.

3. Stain Pre-Treater
   Ingredients: Dish soap
   How: Apply directly to the stain. Work in gently with fingers. Leave
   5 minutes before adding to the wash. Effective on food, grease, and grass.

4. Protein Stain Remover (Blood, Sweat, Egg)
   Ingredients: Cold water + dish soap
   How: COLD WATER ONLY — hot water sets protein stains permanently.
   Rinse under cold water first, then apply dish soap and work in before washing.

5. Whitening Agent (White Fabrics)
   Ingredients: ½ cup baking soda + sunlight
   How: Add baking soda to the wash cycle. After washing, dry whites in
   direct sunlight. Sun naturally bleaches and whitens fabric.

6. Wash Odour Eliminator
   Ingredients: ½ cup baking soda
   How: Add directly to the drum before adding clothes. Neutralises sweat
   and body odour in the wash. Particularly effective for sportswear.

7. Washing Machine Cleaner
   Ingredients: 2 cups white vinegar
   How: Run an empty hot cycle with vinegar. Also wipe the rubber door seal
   with undiluted vinegar — mould builds up in the folds. Leave door open
   after every wash to prevent future mould growth.

8. Colour Brightener (Dark Clothes)
   Ingredients: ½ cup white vinegar in rinse cycle
   How: Prevents dark colours from fading by removing detergent residue
   that dulls fabric over time. Same method as fabric softener.

9. Delicates Hand Wash
   Ingredients: A few drops of castile soap in cool water
   How: Fill a basin with cool water, add soap. Gently agitate delicates
   by hand. Rinse thoroughly. Never wring — press gently to remove water.

10. Towel Mildew Refresh
    Ingredients: Baking soda + white vinegar (two separate cycles)
    How: Cycle 1 — wash with ½ cup baking soda, no detergent.
    Cycle 2 — wash again with ½ cup white vinegar, no detergent.
    Fully removes mildew smell from towels that have gone stale.

CATEGORY 5: OUTDOOR AREAS (7 Solutions)
════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Outdoor Furniture Cleaner
   Ingredients: Warm water + dish soap
   How: Scrub with a brush or cloth. Rinse with a hose. Works on plastic,
   metal, and wicker furniture.

2. Mould on Walls or Outdoor Tiles
   Ingredients: Undiluted white vinegar
   How: Spray generously. Leave for 1 hour. Scrub with a stiff-bristled brush
   and rinse with water. Repeat for heavy mould.

3. Barbecue Grill Degreaser
   Ingredients: Baking soda + dish soap (paste)
   How: Apply paste to grill grates while still warm (not hot). Leave 15 minutes.
   Scrub with a grill brush. Rinse with hot water.

4. Garden Tool Cleaner
   Ingredients: 50/50 white vinegar and water
   How: Soak tools for 30 minutes. Scrub off dirt and rust. Dry fully
   before storage to prevent further rusting.

5. Patio Concrete Cleaner
   Ingredients: Baking soda + warm water
   How: Sprinkle baking soda generously on the patio surface. Scrub with
   a stiff brush and warm water. Rinse off with a hose.

6. Outdoor Bin Deodoriser
   Ingredients: Hot soapy water + baking soda
   How: Rinse bin interior with hot soapy water. Allow to dry. Sprinkle
   baking soda at the base before replacing the bin bag.

7. Rust Remover (Metal Tools & Fixtures)
   Ingredients: Undiluted white vinegar
   How: Soak rusted items overnight in vinegar. The acetic acid breaks down
   rust. Scrub with steel wool the next morning and rinse thoroughly.

CATEGORY 6: SPECIAL AREAS & OBJECTS (11 Solutions)
════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Mattress Refresher
   Ingredients: Baking soda + optional essential oil
   How: Strip the bed. Sprinkle baking soda over the entire mattress surface.
   Leave for at least 1 hour (longer = better). Vacuum off completely.
   Do this every 3 months. Rotate the mattress at the same time.

2. Pillow Cleaner
   Ingredients: ½ cup baking soda
   How: Wash pillows in the washing machine on a gentle cycle with baking soda.
   Tumble dry fully — any remaining moisture causes mould. Wash every 3 months.

3. Shoe Deodoriser
   Ingredients: Baking soda
   How: Sprinkle inside shoes before bed. Leave overnight. Tip out before
   wearing. For persistent odour, repeat nightly for a week.

4. Rubbish Bin Deodoriser
   Ingredients: Baking soda + essential oil + cotton ball
   How: Drop a few drops of essential oil onto a cotton ball, then sprinkle
   baking soda over it. Place at the bottom of the bin before inserting the bag.

5. Car Interior Cleaner
   Ingredients: White vinegar + water spray
   How: Spray diluted vinegar onto a microfibre cloth (not directly on surfaces).
   Wipe dashboard, door panels, and cup holders. Eliminates odours and bacteria.

6. Sponge Sanitiser
   Ingredients: Water (for microwave method) OR white vinegar
   How: Method 1 — wet the sponge fully and microwave for 60 seconds.
   Method 2 — soak in undiluted white vinegar for 5 minutes, rinse.
   Replace sponges every 2 weeks regardless.

7. Aircon Filter Cleaner
   Ingredients: Warm water
   How: Remove the filter panel. Rinse under warm running water to dislodge
   dust. Air dry fully before replacing — never replace a damp filter.
   Clean monthly in Singapore's humidity.

8. Sticky Residue Remover (Labels, Tape)
   Ingredients: A few drops olive oil OR rubbing alcohol
   How: Apply to the residue, leave 1 minute. Rub with a dry cloth.
   Olive oil for gentle surfaces; rubbing alcohol for harder surfaces.

9. Jewellery Cleaner
   Ingredients: Warm water + a few drops of dish soap
   How: Soak jewellery for 10 minutes. Use a soft toothbrush to gently
   scrub crevices. Rinse under warm water. Pat dry with a soft cloth.
   Do NOT use on porous stones like pearls or opals.

10. Microfibre Cloth Care
    Ingredients: Warm water (no fabric softener)
    How: Machine wash in warm water. NEVER use fabric softener — it clogs the
    microfibre structure and makes the cloth ineffective. Air dry or tumble dry low.

11. Fridge Water Filter Reminder
    Note: Replace every 6 months. Signs it needs changing: slow water flow,
    unusual taste, or cloudy water. Mark the date on a sticker inside the fridge.

CATEGORY 7: NATURAL INGREDIENT GUIDE
════════════════════════════════════════════════

These 10 ingredients are behind almost every solution in this guide.
Cheap. Safe. Effective. No harsh chemicals.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHITE VINEGAR
Best for: Descaling, deodorising, killing mould and bacteria, mineral deposits
Avoid on: Marble, granite, natural stone, cast iron (acid damages these surfaces)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
BAKING SODA
Best for: Absorbing odours, mild abrasive scrubbing, whitening, fizzing action
Avoid on: Already-clean surfaces without rinsing — leaves white residue if not removed
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
DISH SOAP
Best for: Cutting grease, all-purpose cleaning, stain pre-treatment
Avoid on: Delicate fabrics directly; do not mix with bleach (toxic fumes)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
LEMON JUICE
Best for: Sanitising, deodorising, cutting grease, naturally whitening
Avoid on: Marble and natural stone (acidic — etches the surface permanently)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
HYDROGEN PEROXIDE (3%)
Best for: Disinfecting, blood and mould stain removal, whitening white fabrics
Avoid on: Coloured fabrics without testing first — can bleach; avoid on wood
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
CASTILE SOAP
Best for: Gentle all-purpose cleaner, laundry, hand washing delicates
Avoid on: Mixing directly with vinegar — the acid and base neutralise each other
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
RUBBING ALCOHOL (ISOPROPYL, 70%)
Best for: Ink stains, adhesive residue, electronics and screens
Avoid on: Painted surfaces and finished wood without testing — can strip finish
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
OLIVE OIL
Best for: Wood polishing, sticky residue removal, stainless steel buffing
Avoid on: Fabric surfaces and porous materials — can leave an oil stain
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
COARSE SALT
Best for: Abrasive scrubbing on cutting boards and pots, natural pest deterrent
Avoid on: Delicate surfaces, non-stick coatings, and finished wood
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACTIVATED CHARCOAL
Best for: Passive odour absorption in enclosed spaces (wardrobes, shoes, cars)
Avoid on: Not a surface cleaner — use only as a passive absorber in bags or dishes
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
