## [1:10 – 2:00] WHAT YOU'LL NEED

[B-roll: Flat lay of cleaning supplies on a clean countertop — microfibre cloths, spray bottles, a mop, vacuum, gloves.]

**NARRATION:**
"Before we clean a single thing, let's talk tools. You don't need a cabinet full of products. You need the right ones.

Here's your starter kit:

Number one — microfibre cloths. At least four of them. These are the single most important thing in your cleaning kit. They trap dust and bacteria without spreading it around.

Number two — an all-purpose spray cleaner. One good bottle handles most surfaces in your home.

Number three — a toilet cleaner and a toilet brush. Non-negotiable.

Number four — a mop or steam mop for hard floors.

Number five — a vacuum cleaner, even a basic one.

And number six — rubber gloves. Protect your hands, especially when working with stronger cleaners.

That's it. Six things. You don't need fifteen different sprays for fifteen different surfaces. Keep it simple."

[On-screen text: List of 6 items appearing one by one as narration reads them]

## [2:00 – 2:45] THE GOLDEN RULES OF CLEANING

[B-roll: Overhead shot of someone wiping a shelf, dust falling downward. Then sweeping the floor last.]

**NARRATION:**
"Before we go room by room, there are two rules that will make every clean faster and more effective.

Rule one: always clean top to bottom. Start with high surfaces — shelves, countertops, tables — and work your way down to the floor. Dust and debris fall downward. If you vacuum first and then wipe the shelves, you're just putting dirt back on your clean floor.

Rule two: dry before wet. Dust and dry surfaces first, then use your sprays and damp cloths. If you spray a dusty surface, you're creating a muddy paste that's harder to remove.

Top to bottom. Dry before wet. Remember these two rules and your cleans will be twice as effective."

[On-screen text: "RULE 1: Top to Bottom" → "RULE 2: Dry Before Wet"]

## [2:45 – 5:00] THE KITCHEN

[B-roll: Clean kitchen footage — wiping countertops, cleaning stovetop, scrubbing sink, wiping down appliances.]

**NARRATION:**
"Let's start with the kitchen — the room that usually needs the most attention.

Step one: clear the surfaces. Move everything off your countertops. Dishes into the sink, small appliances to the side. You want a clear workspace before you start cleaning.

Step two: wipe the top surfaces first. Shelves, the top of the fridge, the top of your cabinets if you can reach. Use a dry microfibre cloth and let the dust fall.

Step three: tackle the stovetop. This is where most people give up, but it doesn't have to be hard. Spray your all-purpose cleaner, let it sit for 30 seconds — that's important, let the cleaner do the work — then wipe in circular motions with your cloth. For stubborn grease, a second application and a little more pressure is all it takes.

Step four: countertops. Spray and wipe from back to front, moving towards the sink. Don't forget the backsplash behind the stove and the handles of your cabinets — these collect more grease than anywhere else in the kitchen.

Step five: the sink. Spray it, scrub it, rinse it. A clean sink makes the whole kitchen feel clean.

Step six: the floor. Sweep first to collect loose debris, then mop. Start from the far end of the kitchen and work your way towards the exit — you don't want to step on your clean floor as you go.

The kitchen is done. Move on."

---

## [5:00 – 7:00] THE BATHROOM

[B-roll: Cleaning bathroom mirror, wiping sink, scrubbing toilet, cleaning shower/bathtub, mopping floor.]

**NARRATION:**
"The bathroom. A lot of people dread this one. But with the right order, it takes less than fifteen minutes.

Step one: the mirror. Spray a little of your all-purpose cleaner directly onto a dry microfibre cloth — not onto the mirror — and wipe in an S-pattern from top to bottom. Wiping in circles causes streaks.

Step two: the sink and vanity. Spray, wipe, rinse. Pay attention to the tap and handles — these are high-touch areas that carry the most bacteria.

Step three: the toilet. Apply your toilet cleaner inside the bowl and let it sit while you clean the outside first. Wipe the tank, the lid, the seat — both sides — and the base. Then scrub the inside of the bowl and flush. Outer surface first, bowl last. Always.

Step four: the shower or bathtub. Spray your cleaner on the walls and floor of the shower, let it sit for 30 seconds, then scrub with a cloth or brush. Pay attention to corners and grout lines — that's where mould starts.

Step five: the floor. Same as the kitchen — sweep first, then mop.

Your bathroom is done. And it took you less time than you thought."

---

## [7:00 – 8:30] THE BEDROOM

[B-roll: Making a bed with crisp sheets, dusting bedside table, vacuuming floor, wiping windowsill.]

**NARRATION:**
"The bedroom is where you sleep — which means the quality of this space directly affects the quality of your rest.

Step one: make the bed first. This sounds simple, but it changes the feel of the entire room. A made bed makes everything else look tidier before you've even cleaned anything.

Step two: declutter. Pick up anything that doesn't belong — clothes on the floor, items on your bedside table. Put them in their place. Cleaning and decluttering are different things. Clear the room before you start cleaning it.

Step three: dust all surfaces. Bedside tables, dresser, shelves, the top of your wardrobe. Use your dry microfibre cloth. Top to bottom, remember.

Step four: wipe down surfaces with your damp cloth. Bedside table, windowsill, light switches.

Step five: vacuum or sweep the floor. Move under the bed too — that's where dust accumulates most.

Step six: open your window for five minutes after you're done. Fresh air finishes the job."

---

## [8:30 – 9:45] THE LIVING ROOM

[B-roll: Fluffing and arranging cushions, dusting shelves and TV unit, vacuuming sofa and floor, wiping remote controls.]

**NARRATION:**
"The living room is usually the first thing people see when they walk into your home. It sets the tone for everything.

Step one: reset the furniture. Straighten the cushions, fold any throws, push chairs back into position. This alone changes the feel of the room.

Step two: dust from top to bottom. Ceiling fan if you have one, then shelves, TV unit, coffee table. Wipe the TV screen gently with a dry microfibre cloth — no spray.

Step three: wipe down high-touch surfaces. Remote controls, light switches, door handles. These are cleaned the least and touched the most.

Step four: vacuum the sofa. Use the attachment nozzle to get into the crevices — crumbs and dust collect there.

Step five: vacuum or sweep and mop the floor. Move furniture slightly to get the edges and corners.

Living room done."

---

## [9:45 – 10:30] BUILDING YOUR ROUTINE

[B-roll: Calm, satisfying montage of a fully clean home — each room looking fresh and bright.]

**NARRATION:**
"Now here's the thing — you don't need to do all of this every day. The goal is a system, not a sprint.

Daily: spend ten minutes resetting. Make your bed, wipe the kitchen counter after cooking, and do the dishes. That's it. Ten minutes a day prevents the big build-up.

Weekly: pick one or two rooms to do a proper clean. Rotate through your home. By the end of the week, everything has been done.

Monthly: the deep clean. Windows, behind appliances, inside the fridge, grout lines.

Daily resets. Weekly cleans. Monthly deep cleans. That's the system.

When you build this into your life, your home stops being something you deal with — and starts being something that works for you."
